Timber Wolf

When the pack howls together, the harmony of their voices can make it sound like the pack is larger than it is.

  • Scientific Name: Canis lupus lycaon
  • Habitat: Wilderness
  • Average Size: 3-5 ft; 95-100 lbs
  • Diet: Carnivore

Timber wolves are the largest living wild canine species. They have large canine teeth, powerful jaws, and keen senses that help them hunt large hoofed mammals like deer, elk, bison, and moose, as well as smaller mammals like beavers, rodents, and hares. They can also run up to 37 miles per hour when pursuing prey.
